Early Life

Minaria is the daughter of the gods Persephone and Hermes. Persephone was only a few months pregnant when she had to return to the Underworld at the end of summer. To hide her infidelity from Hades, she made herself lose the pregnancy just before the autumn equinox. It would have been a miscarriage, but of course, the child of two gods is immortal. Newborn Mina drifted in the Ionian Sea with her afterbirth until, suddenly, it broke apart in the water and formed a small island.

The baby and the island grew together. At first, it was bare except for a willow tree, from which she suckled sap. As days and years went by, the land slowly filled with plants and wildlife. Even the land itself expanded in size as Mina did. Hermes came to the island bearing a message for Mina when she was about five years old. He told her, with some regret, that she would have to leave the island. The gods, especially Zeus and Demeter, did not want her so close to the eyes and ears of Olympus where her discovery might invoke Hades' wrath. Mina grieved for her island, but agreed that she should move on to the land of mortals. Hermes carried her to the coast of Italy and sent her on her way. He also gave her a surname that she could use in the mortal realm: Theopolis, from theós (god) and pólis (city). Her middle name, Amona (from m√≥na, alone), she gave to herself at a later age.

Youth and Death

Like most gods, Min was mature and self-reliant almost from the time she was a baby. She left the caravan to continue heading north, away from Greece, and made her way alone all the way into northern Germany. A farmer offered her a ride into Hamburg and brought her to an orphanage.

Min tried to run away from the Hamburg orphanage several times, but she didn't have a way out of the city; someone always caught her. She also found a reason to stay: Maja, Danny, Lana, and Eliza. The orphanage was an unhappy place, but she decided to bide her time until they could find somewhere for all of them to escape together.

After a particularly nasty day when Min was 12, she ran away from the orphanage again, and finally managed to leave the city unseen. She travelled several miles until she came to a large estate owned by the wealthy Hüber family, which bordered on a forest. At the edge of the property, she found three-year-old Sofia, who had slipped away from her nurse, playing with a snake. Min coaxed the snake away and brought Sofia back to the big house. To show their gratitude, the Hübers let her stay for a night. Min liked the family, and convinced the cook to give her work as a kitchen maid. Sofia, however, did not forget about her hero. She pleaded to spend time with Min so often that Min became her nurse instead.

Min and Sofia became inseparable, and the other Hüber sisters Elisabeth and Olya began to think of her as a friend. Min wrote occasional letters to the Hamburg orphanage, careful to make sure the headmistress didn't find out. Min was still restless, but she loved Sofia too much to leave. The winter when Sofia was six and Min 16, Sofia fell into a fast-moving river. Min carried her out as fast as she could, but between her injuries and hypothermia, Sofia was going to die. Spring came early and lasted long that year. As Min healed, she explored every inch of the islands and experimented with the limits of her own powers. Faced with the reality of her identity as a god, she made an effort to connect with her original family, but her mother remained out of reach. Hermes, always busy, didn't make much time to talk to her, but he did introduce her to his other young offspring, her half-brother Heredes. Min invited Heredes to visit her islands during the day, and Gethos came at nightfall.

Gethos fell in love with her easily, but he was cautious to say anything. She fell in love more slowly, but with equal intensity. Their relationship was consensual, but there were always complexities in their difference in age and power, as well as lingering questions for Min over Sofia's fate. It became unhealthy when Min got pregnant, and Gethos refused the possibility of raising their children among mortals. He thought her feelings for him would overpower her love for her mortal companions, but her heart was committed to saving Sofia. She wanted to return to life with Maja and the other orphans. They fought over their future for months; eventually, she refused to see him. She spent more time with Heredes, who helped her begin building what would eventually by the Seezo Haus. She went into labor too early, as her mother had, and briefly held her twins, Akonitia and Mekonos, before Gethos took them away and left her with Sofia.

Seezo

When Min left the islands with Sofia and Heredes, she discovered that they were no longer located in the Ionian Sea, but instead just off the coast of the Jutland Peninsula. They spent a few days in the coastal village of Hafen, ejoying the kindness of Alfred and Else Hofer and young Vera Trumbauer. Then they spent the fall reconnecting with friends in the mortal realm: Elizabeth and Olya, Maja, Danny, Lana, and Eliza. Each of them agreed to join the group in their new home on the islands. They stayed the winter with the Hofers and moved to the island in the spring. After Seezo

As Min grew into her power, her affect on those around her also grew. Too many lost children came to Hafen. Too many of the people she loved felt melancholy and restless. Min's job is to let people confront their grief and learn to live with it, and to protect children when they have no other family. She is not meant to be in people's lives for very long. After many decades on Seezo, the family the orphans had created began to drift apart. Danny married Vera. Lana and Eliza moved to the mainland. Olya and Johan went to Denmark. Sofia asked to return to the Underworld. Elisabeth went to Hamburg. Heredes returned to Olympus, and tried to make Min follow him. She refused. She and Maja decided to travel.
