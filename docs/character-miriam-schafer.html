miriam Schäfer

Curly dark brown hair, blue eyes, muscular, blue and red dirndl with a shawl from Johanna; intense and introspective

Born in Germany and raised by foster mother named Johanna Schäfer, who was a jewish weaver and shepherd from the Jutland Peninsula. Johanna died in a fistfight at a pub in a nearby village while Miriam, who was 5, was watching the sheep. When the authorities came to take her back to the orphanage, she hid from them (with Johanna's mule, Elijah) and was later found on the run by a farmer and his wife, who knew Min and convinced Miriam to trust her.

She doesn't always say what's on her mind, but if she has something to say she's never shy about it and can come across as harsh or blunt. Around her closest friends (Joceline, Sabah, and Sofia) she is much more open and outgoing. She used to be quite short, but had a big growth spurt; however, her personality always makes her seem bigger than she really is.

She learned a lot of her blunt behavior from Johanna, who was neglectful, often drunk, and abusive at times. She was very curious about where she came from before Johanna, but she ended up with a beating more often than a straight answer. Miriam still respected her authority, but she's been bitter ever since finding a more sympathetic mother in Min. She still keeps an enormous wool shawl that Johanna wove for her- blue and red with white patterns.

Johanna tasked her to go out and watch the sheep during the day, which she enjoyed greatly for the solitude. She also grew very fond of the mule Elijah, who always hee-hawed and wanted to graze with the flock- he was only allowed out to work since he was so stubborn around hard-fisted Johanna, so Miriam stole him in the end. One year for her adoption day, Henry carved a flock of sheep, like in the nativity scene.

She's not one for sitting still and reading; she can sew and knit, but much prefers to do so standing up.

She likes to sing the old German and Jewish songs that Johanna taught her- she and Sabah will sit outside with the sheep singing for ages if they can get away with it. She also has a harmonica that Johanna taught her to play.

She doesn't consider herself Jewish, but she follows many Jewish practices because that's how Johanna raised her.
