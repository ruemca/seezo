sabah bint-Yahya

Slavery in Germany is not as pervasive as in other parts of the world, or later in history, but Sabah is certainly a racial minority and Min takes care to protect of her, and doesn't let her go to town alone. Sabah has become increasingly self-concious of race with age, and she's had some discussions with Min and Maja about it; however, no one on Seezo takes much notice of her skin color after a while. For some of the younger foreign children who'd never seen a black person, it was a curiosity initially, but wore off quickly.

She left her mother too young to know much about North African culture or Islam, but she still wears a headscarf because that's how her mom always looked. She has a small scrap of green fabric torn from the hem of her mother's dress.

She doesn't know her exact birthday, so she picked the first day she ever saw snow. She was so excited and curious and wouldn't stop staring out the window at the flakes coming down. She's gotten assorted gifts, including a book of Arabian Nights tales and small jewelry. She adores the wintertime because of the snow, and wants to go outside when almost no one else will to visit the goats and run around. However, she also gets cold easily, and snuggles up in blankets in the evening before bed, and wears fingerless gloves most of the time.

She loves to go out and watch the sheep with Miriam or by herself, and usually sings with the birds or to the sheep.

She took Ania under her wing when she arrived because Alexei couldn't. Ania had so much energy and they immediately clicked, and she would take her around the farm and they would watch the goats together. Later as Alexei began to recover they spent time together, so she can tease him and cheer him up.

She taught Miriam the Arabic and Italian mixture that she remembers, and Miriam taught her some Yiddish, so they can communicate in their own secret pidgin language when they want. Ania has also picked up some of the words, so sometimes only they can fully understand her.
